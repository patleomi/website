What I can help with

I want to help you get unstuck when performance costs are causing you to burn out. Instead of randomly taking a new job that risks creating the same conditions, we can think through what you need to ask yourself and employers to find the places where you're a better fit, and how to talk about yourself in ways that convinces them the fit is mutual.

My favorite example is the farmer gone coder. Most hiring managers won't be convinced that sowing seeds or laying hydroponics are transferrable skills. What would be convincing is someone who can describe how their self-taught systems thinking, troubleshooting, and solution building skills are the same one's they'll need to rely on to program effectively. It's a different domain, but the skills themselves transfer.

How I build sessions & talks

Whether I'm invited to give a talk or we're working one-on-one or in small groups, we'll start with a similar strategy:

Define the Problem

We'll schedule a free 15-minute consult to kick off a mutual fit exploration, and then if we agree to proceed, we'll meet again to do a deep dive into the problem and what success looks like.

Build

We'll work through an action plan together. If I'm being invited for a talk, I'll do all the building for you. If I'm providing coaching, we'll co-discover what you need to build yourself.

Test & Reflect

You test what you learned, see what sticks, and come back if you need clarification. I offer retainer support for difficult questions. I want you independent of me quickly.

What makes a fit

I get along best with certain working styles and expectations. Below is what that looks like. If you see yourself, let's talk.

What you'll bring

  • A feeling that something isn't working, even if you can't name it.
  • A drive to have difficult conversations you can act on.
  • A readiness to do the kind of career development that requires deep thinking.

What you want to happen

  • Be able to ask the questions to help identify the environments that fit your style.
  • Understand how to communicate in ways where people and AI understand what you need.
  • Know how to continue learning and growing when I'm not around.

Who you definitely aren't

  • Someone who wants others to do the work for them.
  • A person who expects to use AI as an easy button or thinking shortcut.
  • One who needs feel good feedback over meaningful but sometimes hard to hear truths.
