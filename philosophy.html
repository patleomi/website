How I Coach

Matching a coach whose style fits yours matters for building the trusting relationship that will lead you to your goals. This page highlights some of how I approach coaching.

On Professionalism

I don't buy into traditional, performative professionalism. Optics matter so much less to me than making sure each and every one of us are building the communities we actually want to be a part of. We can't do that if we have to hide the hard parts for the sake of the company or whatever it is we're involved with. That's a recipe for burnout. As long as we're each communicating effectively and with mutual respect, I don't care how you say it.

On Authenticity

Put another way, traditional professionalism prevents people from communicating what they actually mean by stunting authenticity. We're going to waste a lot of time talking around the problem if you're prefiltering it through carefully chosen words that mask meaning. I'd rather you express yourself in a way that comes natually, assume good intentions, and work through what you actually mean until we understand what's actually at the core.

On Privilege

It's hard to be authentic if we can't name the elephant in the room. I'm a cis, straight, white, male, American veteran, first-generation, neurodivergent college graduate with experience as a transfer student. What's worked for and against me will be different from what might work for and against you. When we think about your situation, we need to be able to talk about identity if we want to find the tools and strategies that are appropriate for you. I'll share what's helped me, and I'll acknowledge when I think my privilege has influenced that process.

On Politics

Whether you like it or not, politics are a professional issue. Conversations about how work influences you can only go so far if we act like political forces don't exist. We can't talk about how hard it is to find jobs right now if we can't talk about Trump. We can't identify barriers you might experience as an international applicant without also talking about racism, xenophobia, and American exceptionalism. We can't understand the environments that support you and your identities if we can't discuss diversity, equity, and inclusion. I'm not here to influence your politics. You're influenced by these issues whether or not we talk about them, and we can't identify an effective strategy to cut through the bullshit if we pretend these things don't exist.

On Directness

My coaching style depends on my ability to deliver support directly. By direct I don't mean rude or abrasive. I care deeply about people, and I believe it's harmful to you to make you feel good in the moment with praise that isn't supported by the evidence. I'll always tell you when I think something is or isn't working against you, explain why, and then we'll figure out next steps together. You can leave our sessions clear on where you stand with an action plan and an understanding of exactly why each step matters.
