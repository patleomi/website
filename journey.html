How I Got Here

I've spent my life online learning how to read what people actually mean to say through text. I translate that skill into how I help people talk about their career, in writing, during interviews, and with generative AI chatbots.

Professional Journey

Arc 1: Where I Started

Pre-2008

I'm a first-generation digital native.

My Experience

I'm not sure who I'd be without technology and the internet. I spent hours by myself as a kid while my parents painted houses in the summer. Dial up internet gave me some of my first real connections to people and the world, and Google gave me a way to teach myself anything I didn't know.

I spent my teens building my social life around understanding how people talk and think in text. I saw the evolution of chat rooms to instant messengers to MySpace and Facebook, and through T9 to Smartphones. My social life depended on learning how to be visible in a digital space. It made me a more critical reader. I learned to always look for what people weren't saying just as much as what they were.

Impact on My Coaching

When we talk about your career, I'm always trying to understand what you're saying in the context of what I know about you so far. Being a digital native taught me that. I think about whether what you say matches what I think you mean, and then redirect us toward that. This same thinking can help you communicate with AI tools more clearly.

Arc 2: Interpersonal Compass

2008 to 2016

People trust me because they know I say what I mean and always explain why.

My Experience

I joined the Army at 18 under a high-risk contract committing me to four years active duty on combat-deployable teams with no option to finish in the inactive reserves. I spent time in Germany, Iraq, and Arizona before going back to Washington State for the final four years in the National Guard.

Having social democratic values in the military usually means either assimilating or risking alienation. I chose another path. I engaged people, asked difficult questions directly, confronted others when I thought they were wrong, and talked them through why I thought that way. I built relationships not because I changed minds but because I showed other people I was consistently authentic.

Being in the Army also refined my thinking as a digital native. I joined the Signal Corps as a cable installer, where my job was obsolete by the time I landed at my first duty station. To maintain relevance, I learned about IT networking and troubleshooting technology so that I could support a help desk. I refined my help desk skills during the Guard while I also worked in a restaurant training other servers.

Impact on My Coaching

You won't walk away from our conversations wondering if I meant what I said or if I'm just trying to make you feel confident. I'll help you build that confidence through honesty, telling you what seems to be working and what isn't, so that you'll always have a clear direction at the end of every conversation. If you leave unsure, I'm not doing my job.

Working on a help desk and waiting tables helped me understand that the problems you might be having might not be the problems that you think they are. Naming observations directly lets us figure out what the real problem is. Whether that's you incorrectly blaming your resume or the AI you're trying to use, we'll talk until we figure out what might actually be driving the problem.

Arc 3: Research Foundations

2016 to 2019

I'm known for asking questions with measurable answers.

My Experience

I earned two promotions in my first professional job by automating the most time consuming parts. Both times, I asked about whether the limitations of our work were the processes themselves or the assumptions the humans behind them were making. My military experience gave me the foundation to ask those questions, but my undergrad years in psychology helped me measure the results and my time as a researcher let me be systematic about it. I read dozens of studies to operationalize a research question and measure how it showed up in other studies, and I spent a few years translating science for the public.

Being a researcher also introduced me to programming logic. I helped update an assessment tool to match changes to its mental health diagnostic criteria by tracing the old logic and revising it so that a programmer could implement the changes.

Impact on My Coaching

We need to be able to measure your problem to know what success looks like. If we can't, how will you know when you've met your goals? Being clear about what you need allows you to be more systematic, enabling you to test if strategies are serving you rather than assume they're working without exploring if they actually do. This same thinking lends itself to how you use AI. You'll find the outcomes much more relevant and tailored when you're clear about what you actually expect to happen.

Arc 4: Educating & Systems Thinking

2019 to Summer 2023

I translate confusing ideas.

My Experience

I earned a Master of Public Health during the pandemic at a time when the federal government was actively denying my field's existence. Most people want to do the things that help them. What gets in their way is an abundance of misinformation and misplaced messaging. I learned how to reduce those barriers during a writing fellowship where my sole responsibility was finding new research and communicating what people need to know about it from a health perspective. I realized that the key skill people need to develop is translating what they know in a way other people can understand.

When I became a psychology instructor for a semester, I taught this skill. I built a semester project around teaching students how to teach other people about psychology. Around the same time, I took on a role in a Communication Lab designing and running sessions for life scientists on elevator pitches, research presentations, and application development, all centered around that idea of translation.

Impact on My Coaching

I've taught for long enough to know that I can't just give you something and assume it works. The hardest part of learning is learning from someone who's an ineffective teacher. I work to make everything I do as accessible as possible, so that your only barrier to learning is the complexity of the material itself. I check in frequently to probe for different ways I can adapt instruction, and I want to know the real answer.

What I'm doing here is leading by example. I need you to learn this same skill. You might have incredible experiences, but those experiences are meaningless if you can't communicate them to an AI or a hiring manager in a way that gives them the information they need. You can't get there if you're not open to continuous growth.

Arc 5: Coaching & Prompting

Fall 2023 to Now

I lean into every part of who I am when I coach others.

My Experience

Learning how to talk to generative AI became my first real hobby in 2023. It merged a lifetime of thinking in digital voice, overcoming communication obstacles, and asking deep questions, all with an audience that has the time and patience to let me practice endlessly.

I first started using these tools when I applied to a Doctor of Education program. ChatGPT showed me an example of something I couldn't find online, and I've been glued to it ever since. I took a year of courses in that program, studying workplace learning to understand how to teach generative AI. I left when I started working for Northeastern University's Career Development office and realized that I could learn more through applied daily practice than in the program.

My time at Northeastern refined my teaching and coaching style. I spent countless hours both at work and home thinking through how job seekers could benefit from generative AI. I built a workshop series around it and constantly reflected on what teaching AI really means. My name traveled through word of mouth, leading to invitations to give talks across the university for students, staff, and faculty. I also served as an AI Champion, contributing best practices to their AI Working Group. I now bring all of it into my own private practice.

Impact on My Coaching

Everything here builds toward the same message. As a communication coach, I ask the questions that we can measure to see if any of our conversations are truly actionable. I help you translate the complicated parts of your career so that employers believe you when you say you're relevant. I'm consistently direct, so that you trust that what I'm saying is real. I read you for the voice behind the voice. These skills are the same skills that make someone effective at using generative AI, because using AI well depends on communicating clearly enough that something useful happens.

You're always in control of how much we talk about using AI in your career. My goal is to help you get clarity, regardless of how we get there. Then I'll teach you how to maintain that clarity throughout your career, so that you can keep growing after our sessions end.
