Who I Am

I'm a communication coach who wants to help people who are tired of performing at work.

Whether you're masking, code switching, or desperately trying to fit in because it never quite feels right, some people find existing in the professional world especially draining. Work doesn't always have to be like that.

I'm Pat Leomi, and for me the professional world is exhausting. I'm excited about learning and contributing to what I find meaningful, but showing up like myself has always been a struggle. I'm either forcing the fit and losing all energy from masking or being penalized for authenticity.

Traditional professionals would advise me to put my nose down and "do the work" regardless of the harms. That's not how my brain works. I'm compelled to be myself and ask why that's not ok when friction comes up, and then I move toward something new when the answer doesn't make sense. It's not easy, but sometimes finding a new setting and communicating well enough to get accepted is the only way to make it work.

Becoming a career coach was the first place where masking costs felt manageable. I joined Northeastern University as an Assistant Director for Career Development. It was refreshing to find that job seekers and talk audiences cared less about performative professionalism than what they came to learn.

Being an effective coach of any kind requires deep self-reflection. Who am I and why do some environments feel like a misalignment? Those questions led me to learn about PTSD and ADHD, and suddenly fit questions have answers. I've been immersed in places that don't support how my brain works. Now I'm building a career that fits my needs through this private practice.

I've extended my focus from careers to communication coaching more broadly, because I've realized my strongest skill is reading what people mean and helping them be understood. I want to support others like me who don't feel like they fit to find the places and strategies that change it. I'm not a therapist. I'm a deeply reflective communicator who wants to help you learn from my experience so that you can communicate your needs in the settings where they'll be heard. Together, we'll figure out who you are as a professional and how to say it so the right people believe you.
